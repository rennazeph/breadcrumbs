Honey bees are famous for the waggle dance, a behavior used by returning foragers to communicate information about valuable resources. The dance is not a cute extra; it is part of how a colony can direct attention toward flowers, water, or nesting information.

The dance also shows why social behavior is more than crowding many insects together. A colony needs information flow. Foragers must share resource cues, receivers must interpret them, and the colony must adjust as flowers open, weather changes, or competition appears.
