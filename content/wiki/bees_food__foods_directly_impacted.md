Many familiar foods become easier to understand when seen through pollination. Apples, cherries, blueberries, melons, cucumbers, squash, pumpkins, almonds, and many seed crops depend on or benefit from animal pollinators. Bees are often central because they repeatedly visit flowers while collecting pollen and nectar.

The word **depend** needs care. Some crops require insect pollination to set a good crop; others can produce without bees but may produce larger, more uniform, or more numerous fruits when pollination improves. That makes bee relevance a gradient rather than an on-off switch.

In practical food systems, managed honey bees are moved to many crops, while wild bees and other pollinators also contribute. A wiki should show both sides: the agricultural logistics of managed colonies and the ecological value of diverse local pollinator communities.
