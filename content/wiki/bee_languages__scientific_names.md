Scientific names give the wiki a different kind of language. The western honey bee is **Apis mellifera**. Bumble bees belong to the genus **Bombus**. Leafcutter and mason bees are often discussed within **Megachilidae**, while many sweat bees belong to **Halictidae**.

These names are not decorative. They help avoid confusion when common names shift across regions. A common name can describe behavior, appearance, habitat, or a local tradition; a scientific name points to a taxonomic relationship.

For Backpack, scientific names are also a good formatting test. The renderer needs to support italics and inline code-like terms cleanly because wiki pages often mix natural language, labels, lists, and short technical references.
